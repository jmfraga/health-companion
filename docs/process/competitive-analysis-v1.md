## Gap analysis: what does NOT exist

### 1. The market is dominated by REACTIVE tools
Ada, Buoy, Symptomate, K Health, ChatGPT Health = only useful once you have symptoms or YOU ask them.

**Gap**: nobody proactively reaches out to tell you *"You're 45 — it's time for your colonoscopy."*

### 2. Trackers do NOT provide intelligence
Apple Health, Google Fit, MyFitnessPal = cold data repositories.

**Gap**: nobody turns longitudinal data + medical history into personalized prevention plans.

### 3. Coaches are of narrow niche
Noom = weight only. Flo = cycle only. Lark = chronic only (and B2B only).

**Gap**: there is no INTEGRAL health coach (nutrition + exercise + sleep + prevention + screenings + mental health).

### 4. Big Tech is "health search", not "health companion"
ChatGPT, Copilot, Amazon, Perplexity = improved search engines. They do not build a longitudinal relationship.

**Gap**: nobody offers a companion that KNOWS YOU across years and acts as your "health advocate."

### 5. Apple's opportunity is open
Apple cancelled Mulberry (AI Health Coach) in February 2026. Eddy Cue admitted Oura / Whoop do a better job. The space is empty.

### 6. Longitudinality is the unexploited differentiator
Most apps treat every interaction as isolated. Nobody connects *"six months ago your cholesterol was 220, today you're eating more saturated fat, and you stopped walking"* into a continuous narrative.

## Conclusion

The $25B+ market is saturated with reactive symptom checkers and passive trackers. The new Big Tech wave (ChatGPT Health, Copilot, Amazon) are search engines, not companions. **There is no true preventive, proactive, longitudinal health companion for the general consumer.** The cancellation of Apple Health+ confirms that even Apple could not execute this vision. The window of opportunity is now.

The closest competitor is **Galen AI** (YC), but it is ultra early-stage, US / UK / Canada only, and still does not demonstrate proactivity or structured preventive coaching.
