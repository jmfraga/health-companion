GUION ELEVEN LABS — Health Companion · TTS prompt
==================================================

Para usar con tu voz clonada (Voice Lab → Instant Voice Clone o
Professional Voice Clone). Este guion está formateado para el motor
de ElevenLabs — usa <break time> tags para controlar pausa, y los
números delicados se escriben como se pronuncian.

║  CONFIGURACIÓN RECOMENDADA                                       ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                  ║
║  Model:  eleven_multilingual_v2  (mejor para acento neutro       ║
║          inglés con base hispanohablante)                        ║
║                                                                  ║
║  Voice settings:                                                 ║
║    Stability:        0.45   (algo de variación natural)          ║
║    Similarity boost: 0.85   (que se parezca a ti, fuerte)        ║
║    Style exaggeration: 0.20 (sutil — narración, no actuación)    ║
║    Speaker boost:    ON                                          ║
║                                                                  ║
║  Si total dura > 3:00 al renderear, baja Stability a 0.35 y      ║
║  el modelo habla un poco más rápido de forma natural.            ║
║                                                                  ║
║  Genera en UN SOLO take si tu plan lo permite (mejor coherencia  ║
║  prosódica). Si no, separa por las marcas ─── para manejar       ║
║  mejor el tiempo por sección.                                    ║

PROMPT (copia desde aquí hacia abajo, hasta "FIN PROMPT"):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

I'm Juan Manuel Fraga — physician, fifty yesterday, from Mexico. My son is nineteen. <break time="0.4s" /> My parents, fragile in their seventies. <break time="0.5s" /> If I want to be there when he turns fifty, I need to take care of my own health — now.

<break time="1.2s" />

Most people don't notice anything until symptoms appear — by then, the body has been changing for years. <break time="0.6s" /> And that's when the healthcare system shows up. Often, too late. The later it engages, the more complicated and expensive care becomes.

<break time="0.8s" />

I built Health Companion to flip that. <break time="0.4s" /> Not a wellness app. Not another portal. <break time="0.5s" /> A companion that meets you before you're sick — and a system that does better when you stay healthy, not when you don't.

<break time="1.2s" />

Three users in mind: people starting to care for themselves, people already do, and the clinicians who'd rather prevent than treat.

<break time="1s" />

Here's how it works. You start a conversation. The companion captures your story — clinical detail translated into approachable language, no jargon left alone. <break time="0.4s" /> It helps you set goals. Follow up. Build habits. Track progress, with your consent. <break time="0.7s" /> And, most importantly, it builds a longitudinal record of who you are — your health, your habits, your goals — so every conversation ahead is informed by every one before.

<break time="1s" />

It reads from many sources: your conversation, lab PDFs, genetic reports, photos of your wearables and devices. <break time="0.4s" /> Soon, direct integrations with the apps you already use.

<break time="1s" />

But the moat is memory. With Opus four point seven's context window, the companion knows you more deeply each conversation. <break time="0.4s" /> It never replaces your doctor — it makes your time with them count more, and tells you when that time is now.

<break time="1s" />

In the future, it becomes a tool for doctors and clinics that want to do prevention seriously — continuous monitoring, personalized care, early signal when something drifts.

<break time="1s" />

Built by Claude Code subagents, Claude Design, and one human — me. Powered by Opus four point seven's toolkit: tool use, multimodal, extended thinking, prompt caching. <break time="0.5s" /> And built especially for places like Mexico, where most people don't have a family doctor.

<break time="1.2s" />

Because I want more adventures with my son.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIN PROMPT

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  PASOS                                                           ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                  ║
║  1. Voice Lab → Add Voice → Instant Voice Clone (basta con       ║
║     30s de tu voz limpia, sin ruido de fondo, en inglés si       ║
║     puedes — o mezcla EN/ES).                                    ║
║                                                                  ║
║  2. Espera ~30s a que entrene la voz clone.                      ║
║                                                                  ║
║  3. Voice → tu nueva voz → Generate.                             ║
║                                                                  ║
║  4. Pega el bloque entre "PROMPT" y "FIN PROMPT" arriba.         ║
║                                                                  ║
║  5. Voice settings: aplica los valores recomendados arriba.      ║
║                                                                  ║
║  6. Generate. Escucha. Si suena bien, descarga el WAV/MP3.       ║
║                                                                  ║
║  7. En tu editor de video, alinea con los clips Remotion         ║
║     siguiendo los timestamps del archivo "guion grabado.txt".    ║
║                                                                  ║
║  8. Si el audio total dura más de 3 minutos, sube el speaking    ║
║     rate en el editor (1.05× usualmente es invisible al oído).   ║
║                                                                  ║
